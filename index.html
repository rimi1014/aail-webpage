About AAIL

Intelligent Systems for the Future of Aerospace

The Aerospace Autonomy and Intelligence Laboratory (AAIL) is a research group within the Department of Aerospace Engineering at Seoul National University, led by Professor Namhoon Cho.

We develop the theoretical foundations and practical algorithms for autonomous aerospace systems — spanning guidance & control, machine learning, and intelligent decision-making. Our work bridges mathematical rigor with real-world aerospace engineering challenges.

Faculty Page ↗ namhoon.cho@snu.ac.kr

Current Position

Assistant Professor, Department of Aerospace Engineering, Seoul National University. Affiliated with the Artificial Intelligence Institute and Institute of Advanced Aerospace Technology.

Research Focus

Control-theoretic design of optimisation algorithms, robust adaptive control with online model learning, data-driven safe control frameworks, learning-enabled control with safety assurance, and automated control engineering.

Professional Experience

  • Lecturer in Control and Optimisation, Cranfield University (2024–2025)
  • Research Fellow (75th Anniversary Fellowship), Cranfield University (2021–2024)
  • Senior Researcher, Agency for Defense Development (2019–2020)
  • Postdoctoral Fellow, Seoul National University (2017–2019)

Education

  • PhD, Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering, Seoul National University (2012–2017)
    Dissertation: Nonlinear Guidance Law for 3D Path-Following Using Differential Geometry
  • BSc, Mechanical and Aerospace Engineering, Seoul National University (2009–2012)

Honors & Awards
