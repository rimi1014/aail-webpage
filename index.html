Intelligent Systems for the Future of Aerospace

The Aerospace Autonomy and Intelligence Laboratory (AAIL) is a research group within the Department of Aerospace Engineering at Seoul National University, led by Professor Namhoon Cho.

We develop the theoretical foundations and practical algorithms for autonomous aerospace systems — spanning guidance & control, machine learning, and intelligent decision-making. Our work bridges mathematical rigor with real-world aerospace engineering challenges.

Research

What We Work On

Our research spans the theory and application of intelligent autonomous systems.

Theory

Foundations of Control & Learning

  • Concurrent Model Learning & Adaptive Control

    Methods that simultaneously learn system models while adapting control strategies.

  • Safe Control and Learning

    Algorithms with provable safety guarantees during control and learning.

  • Control-Inspired Optimisation

    Applying control theory principles to enhance optimization methods.

  • Scientific Machine Learning for Control

    Neural network architectures incorporating domain knowledge from control theory.

  • Meta-Learning for Control

    Enabling systems to learn how to learn control strategies across problems.

  • Automatic Optimisation of Closed-Loop Systems

    Automating the tuning and optimization of feedback control systems.

Applications

Aerospace & Robotics

  • Guidance, Navigation, and Control (GNC)

    Integrated systems for autonomous vehicle navigation and maneuvering.

  • Trajectory Planning

    Computing feasible and optimal paths for autonomous aerospace systems.

  • Reachability Analysis

    Determining which states are achievable under given safety constraints.

  • Policy Optimisation

    Improving decision-making policies for autonomous systems.

  • Parameter & State Estimation

    Inferring unknown system properties and current states from measurements.
